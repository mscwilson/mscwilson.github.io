About Me

I love making things. Specifically useful things, but I'm not fussy about materials. Wood, fabric, plastic, code - it's all good. I like a challenge.

As a cell biologist, I made solutions, pieces of DNA, and genetically modified cells. Cells are the framework for the original coding languages! DNA, RNA and protein.

I left science to learn new things. I realised that my favourite part of the job was the technical problem-solving. My new career, coding, is about making useful stuff with fun problems and lots to learn.

Now I'm working with computer languages. I'm a graduate engineer at Snowplow Analytics. I live in London, UK.
